Yttri Lab, Student Researcher

This past Fall 2021 semester, I have had the pleasure of joining the Yttri Lab team, studying by computational methods how neural circuits lead to certain behavior. This project is a continuation of a summer project from a previous year. I worked very closely with Alex Hsu, a graduate student in the lab, who directed all my training for the project.

Overall, we aim to understand what constitutes behavior for the brain. My project specifically utilizes closed-loop stimulation to correlate OCD behavior (excessive face-grooming) with neuronal activity.

I have learned to refine pose estimations and program on DeepLabCut to train models that can predict mouse behavior in an open field. The B-SOiD algorithm that Alex Hsu and Dr. Yttri developed was used to identify position patterns of the mouse and discover spontaneous behavior.
